Job Description

Web developers are in some cases overlooked by the outside world, people that don't understand how the web site they use everyday is created and maintained. Some
people may believe that most companies use simple visual applications to create their companies web site. Though this is true in some cases, most companies higher
web developers to work as a team and create web sites that are built to directly reflect the personality and purpose of the company. The tasks these developers may
need to accomplish include scripting languages to build web sites, designing and editing page content, periodic testing of the web site to correct any problems, and
ensuring all content on the web site is compatible with all browser and web formats.





Knowlege and Skills Required for the Job

Web development is a art that may appeal to only a specific type of individual. A person that is persuing web development as a career needs to be absolutly positive that
the trade is right for them. This career requires a great amount of patience, perfectionism, communication, and the will to learn and continue to develope their skills.
Patience comes into play when a project isn't going quite how it was planned. In most cases, there will be a lot of stopages and redundancies in effort to get everything
exactly how it was drawn up, this is also where perfectionism comes in handy. A web site and its appearence is very important to a companies success, thus it is very
important the the web site is built perfect and without and flaws to please the customers. When creating a perfect web site, it will most likely be completed by a team
of people that break each part of the web site into smaller portions, or projects, to make it easier for personnel to focus on one specific part instead of the larger sum.
Communication within the team, as a leader or a member, is vital to the success of the web site. Everyone needs to be on the same page and willing to work together
to complete the project in a timely manner.

On top of the personal skills required to be a good employee and teamate, there are obviously some physical skills that are required to get the job done correctly. These
are skills that are taught and leaned in school, at a boot camp, on line, or simply by individual research, which ever way works best for the individual is up to them.
When it comes to web developement, most of the skills required of the employee are going to be scripting and language based. According to BurningClass.com, the
top five technical skills needed by web developers today include; JavaScript, jQuery, HTML5, PHP, and JAVA. All of these languages can be learned by dedicating a
little personal time out of each day to learning the skills neccisary to become a web developer.




Training and Education Required for the Job

There are many ways to becaome educated on the skills required to become a web developer. In some cases, people prefer to teach themselves the trade though reading books,
watching videos, or just tinkering with different laguages here and there. Although that can be effective for some people, it isn't what most empoyeers are looking for. Most
companies want proof that a person has been formally trained in the art of web development and the languages they are specifically asking for. A job like this will most likely
require a minimum of a two year degree in web development or, more likely, a four year bachalor degree in the Information Technology field. If you are interestedin some of

Salary and Benifits Expected from the Job

When most people think about technology they think about money, nothing is different when considering a web developement degree. According to Will Erstad of Rasmussen
College, the median salary for web developers in 2014 was $63,490, almost $20,000 more than the average personal income in America that year. The numbers don't stop there
either, if someone where to aspire for a career in the field of web developement, they could potentially be in the business of making seven figures as a senior developer. Of course,
it takes a lot of time, dedication, and hard work to reach that level, but it would be worth it, wouldn't it? It really depends on who is in that position. Web development, as stated
earlier, is a skill that can be very frusterating and tedious at times. This career is ment for individuals that legitimately love doing what they do everyday and the money is only a
perk. If someone were to get into web development for the money, the rest of their career may feel like a grind to the top everyday they walk though the door.So if web development
is a career you may want to pursue, take a moment to ask yourself if you want it for the right reasons, or if your actually love developing. Please refer to the graph below to see the
